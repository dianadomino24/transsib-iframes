Steppe и Tayga: THE LONGEST RAILWAY JOURNEY IN THE WORLD

Pies with liver, demob, guides Tanya and Nadia, ryazhenka at the stations, Khabarovsk, Izhevsk, Moscow — 18 days on the road. This way a musician David Bowie was making his way to Europe after a tour from Japan in spring 1973. Bowie was afraid to fly, so he took the train.

In the beginning the usual landscapes of the European part of Russia: fields, cottages, villages, forest belts, power lines. Abramtsevo, Berendeyevo, Rostov the Great, Nerekhta: it seems that you are going either through the country of hobbits, whether in the past, in 1973, and now from the next compartment will come out a tall, ungainly man in a Japanese kimono and bright trousers.

It's time to settle down. In Kostroma, you bought everything you forgot to take with you: instant coffee, vodka, doshik. Make yourself comfortable. We haven't even reached the Urals yet.

The journey finally ceased to be a journey and became trip. You will definitely never forget this mystical experience of diving into the depths of Russia. Europe is left behind, but Asia doesn't think so to begin. You may have been standing still all this time. On the other hand, if you look out the window for a long time (and what else to do), you can see Kolchak's army going to attack the Reds. Or the mammoths that once roamed here along the rails.

Baikal is behind us. You are in the heart of the continent, there is no Greek, no Jew, no Europe, no Asia. The train passes the highest the point of the TRANS-Siberian railway, beyond which the path to the sea begins. Once the road narrowed here to a single track and ran through a narrow tunnel, on the portal of which there was a chthonic indication of the direction: "To the great ocean."

Khabarovsk appears when it already seems that you spend'll spend the rest of your life on the train. You already know more about your fellow passengers than you do about your next of kin. But still some 800 kilometers-and Vladivostok.

Vladivostok

It's like traveling to the Zone in Tarkovsky's Stalker. Something different happens to everyone, but having come this way, no one remains the same person who left Moscow about a week ago.
